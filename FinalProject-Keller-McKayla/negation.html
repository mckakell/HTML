French Negation

In English we form negative sentences by using "not" in a phrase. For example, "I am not an English major". In french, the standard way to form a negative sentence is by using "ne + verb + pas". On this page are some examples of standard negative French sentences and later we will go more in depth.

Standard Form Using Ne + Verb + Pas

In order to make the phrase negative, you surround the active verb with "ne" and pas".
Je parle français → Je ne parle pas français

When the verb used starts with a vowel or a mute h, "ne" becomes "n'".
J'aime le gâteau → Je n'aime pas le gâteau

4 Particular French Negatives

Ne + Verb + Jamais = Never

Elle ne parle jamais = she never talks

Ne + Verb + Plus = No Longer

Elle ne l'aime plus = she is no longer in love with him

Ne + Verb + Personne = No One

Il n'y a personne = there is no one, there isn't anyone

Ne + Verb + Rien = Nothing

Elle n'a rien = she has nothing, she doesn't have anything
