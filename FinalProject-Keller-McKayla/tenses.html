Past, Present & Future

Le Passé Composé

To conjugate the most widely used French past tenses known as 'Le Passé Composé' we will need two verbs to conjugate it. The first verb is an auxiliary verb (Avoir or Être) that we conjugate in the present tense. Then we will need the past participle of the second verb to conjugate the passé composé.

Avoir
(present tense)
J'ai
Tu as
Il a
Elle a
Nous avons
Vous avez
Ils ont
Elles ont
Etre
(present tense)
Je suis
Tu es
Il est
Elle est
Nous sommes
Vous êtes
Ils sont
Elles sont

-ER → É (parlé)

-IR → I (fini)

-RE → U (vendu)

Le Présent

The French present tense is the equivalent of the English simple present. In French, verbs end in 'ER', 'IR' and 'RE'. To conjugate a verb in the present tense we must change the verb endings.

Regarder Prendre Finir
Je regarde prends finis
Tu regardes prends finis
Il / Elle regarde prend finit
Nous regardons prenons finissons
Vous regardez prenez finissez
Ils / Elles regardent prennent finissent

Le Futur

To form the future tense in French we do not need an auxilary verb. Instead we will add the endings "ai, as, a, ons, ez, ont" to the infinitve of a verb. You may note that the future endings look similarly to the verb avoir in the present tense, which can be useful to remember. Below is an example using the verb parler.

  • Je parlerai
  • Tu parleras
  • Il parlera
  • Nous parlerons
  • Vous parlerez
  • Ils parleront

While forming the future tense may seems simply, many verbs have an irregular root that you will need to learn by heart. The good news is that the endings are always regular.
Below is a table of common irregular verbs and their root for the future tense.

Être → ser → je serai
Avoir → aur → j'aurai
Faire → fer → je ferai
Aller → ir → j'irai
Venir → viendr → je viendrai
Voir → verr → je verrai
Recevoir → recevr → je recevrai
