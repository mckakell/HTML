Indefinite Articles

The indefinite article a/an is used exactly the same in French with un/une. Below are a few examples.

un oiseau → a bird
un frigo → a fridge
une bonne → a maid
une fleur → a flower

Unfortunatley, you will have to memorize which nouns are masculine or feminine. If you are unsure, a good tip to know is that generally words ending in -e are feminine and words ending in a consonant are masculine; however, there are many exceptions to this rule.

Plural Indefinite Articles

Des is a plural indefinite article used when referring to more than a single item or thing. The plural indefinite article is not used when making a general statement about a group, like we see later with plural definite articles.

Mon ami vend des fleurs dans le metro.
My friend sells flowers in the metro.

Je vois des vaches partout.
I see cows everywhere.

Definite Articles

The definite article the is equivalent to le, la and les in French. When we use the in English, a definite article will definitely be used in French. This is not the same the other way around.

For example:
Cockroaches live in the walls
Les blattes vivent dans les murs.

Notice in this example this is a blanket statement made about all cockroaches. In these cases, a definite article is applied in French, but not in English.
